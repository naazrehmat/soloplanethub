# 🎒 Solo Travel Tips

Practical advice for anyone planning their first solo trip.

## Before You Go

- **Research local customs** — small things like greetings, tipping, and dress codes vary a lot by country.
- **Share your itinerary** with a friend or family member, including accommodation details.
- **Get a local SIM or eSIM** for data — helps with maps, translation, and staying reachable.
- **Make digital + physical copies** of important documents (passport, bookings).

## Safety Basics

- Avoid sharing details like where you're staying or how long you're traveling alone with strangers.
- Trust your instincts — if a situation feels off, leave.
- Keep emergency contacts and local embassy info handy.
- Stick to well-lit, populated areas at night, especially in unfamiliar cities.

## Making the Most of It

- Stay in hostels or guesthouses if you want more social interaction.
- Join a free walking tour on day one — great way to get oriented and meet people.
- Don't over-plan every hour. Leaving room for spontaneity often leads to the best experiences.
- Take breaks. Solo travel can be tiring — it's okay to have a slow day.

## Budgeting

- Track daily costs for accommodation, food, and transport separately to spot where you can save.
- Use public transport over taxis where possible.
- Look for free walking tours, free museum days, and city tourist cards for discounts.
