# 🇮🇹 Italy Travel Guide

A quick-reference guide for first-time visitors planning a trip to Italy.

## Best Time to Visit

- **Spring (April–June)** and **Fall (September–October)** offer the best balance of good weather and fewer crowds.
- **Summer (July–August)** is peak season — hot, crowded, and more expensive, especially in cities like Florence and Rome.
- **Winter (November–March)** is quieter and cheaper, great for city-focused trips, though some coastal/island spots shut down for the season.

## Getting Around

Italy's train network connects most major cities efficiently:
- Rome ↔ Florence: ~1.5 hours (high-speed train)
- Florence ↔ Venice: ~2 hours
- Booking in advance (via Trenitalia or Italo) usually gets cheaper fares than buying same-day.

## Cities Worth Visiting

| City | Known For | Ideal Stay |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Rome | History, ruins, food | 3–4 days |
| Florence | Renaissance art, architecture | 2–3 days |
| Venice | Canals, unique atmosphere | 2 days |
| Bologna | Food capital, university city | 1–2 days |
| Turin | Underrated, chocolate, museums | 1–2 days |

## Practical Tips

- Many museums require advance booking, especially in peak season (Uffizi, Accademia).
- Tap water is safe to drink almost everywhere in Italy.
- Tipping is not mandatory — service charge is often included in the bill ("servizio incluso").
- Dress modestly when visiting churches (shoulders/knees covered).

## Money-Saving Tips

- Avoid currency exchange kiosks in tourist areas — use bank ATMs instead.
- Eat where locals eat, away from major squares and landmarks.
- Many cities offer combo tickets for multiple attractions.
